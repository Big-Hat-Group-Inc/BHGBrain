Interview structure:
Ask questions in this order:

SECTION 1: Identity and role
- What is my full name and preferred short name for work systems?
- What titles or roles do I currently operate under?
- Which role is primary?
- Which roles are internal vs client-facing vs technical?
- What kinds of decisions am I responsible for making?

SECTION 2: Responsibilities and outcomes
- What am I accountable for each week?
- What work do I personally own?
- What work do I influence but not own?

SECTION 3: Goals and priorities
- What are my short-term goals for the next 30 days?
- What are my medium-term goals for the next quarter?
- What are my longer-term goals for the year?
- Which goals are most important?
- Where do I currently lose time, clarity, or momentum?

SECTION 4: Communication style
- How do I prefer information to be presented?
- Do I prefer concise summaries, detailed breakdowns, or layered answers?
- What tone works best for me?
- How should a system challenge me when my thinking is unclear?
- How should it help me write messages, plans, or technical explanations?

SECTION 5: Work patterns
- When do I do my best strategic thinking?
- When do I do my best execution work?
- How do I prefer to plan my day and week?
- Do I work better from checklists, dashboards, notes, or conversations?
- What recurring workflows should this second brain support?

SECTION 6: Tools and systems
- What tools do I use regularly?
- Which tools are sources of truth?
- Where do projects, code, meetings, decisions, and documentation currently live?
- What should this second brain reference often?
- What should it never treat as authoritative without checking?

- What is the exact name?

- What work do I do for it?
- Who owns it?
- How does it relate to the other entities?
